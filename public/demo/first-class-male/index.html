Located right across from the Selinsgrove Post Office — hence the name — First Class Male is Duane DeWire's vision of what a barbershop should be.

Not a franchise. Not a rush job. A place where a man can sit down, have a real conversation, and walk out looking sharp. The shop is filled with vintage post office memorabilia that gives it character you won't find anywhere else.

Customers drive in from Hershey and Harrisburg for their cuts. Not because it's the closest shop — because it's the right one.

The Story

Ten Weeks. Doors Locked.
Phone Ringing.

"I have people calling 6-7 times — are you open? Are you open? And we can't."

In March 2020, Duane DeWire closed the doors to First Class Male for the first time since he opened. Like every small business owner, he waited. And waited.

12:01 AM. Green Phase.
Doors Open.

When Snyder County finally went green, Duane didn't wait for morning. He opened at 12:01 AM. The shop was lit up in green. Local officials showed up for a ribbon-cutting. He was booked until 2:30 in the morning.

"We wanted to think outside the box, have a celebration, kind of take the edge off."

He worked straight through the night.

His first customer back, Vincent Stoops, wasn't just there for a haircut. He donated 14 inches of hair to Wigs for Kids.

"It's one of those things where it's so easy to do and make such a big impact that it's hard to say I can, and I shouldn't."

Customers drove from Hershey. From Harrisburg. Not for the closest barbershop — for their barbershop.
